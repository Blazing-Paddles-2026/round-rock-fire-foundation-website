Why legacy donors matter

Founding donors make quiet, practical help possible.

This early stage is about building the structure behind the compassion: governance, confidentiality, donor stewardship, and a reserve that can reduce stress when a family is already carrying enough.

The goal is not recognition. It is a system strong enough to help when a fire family’s ordinary life suddenly divides into before and after.

Donor FAQ

Honest answers, not fine print.

Is my gift tax-deductible?

Yes. The Round Rock Fire Foundation is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit. You’ll receive a tax-deductible receipt by email after each gift. Add your EIN here for donor records.

How are donations processed?

Online gifts are processed through the Central Texas Community Foundation in partnership with Network for Good. Your card or bank information is never stored on our site.

What percentage goes to programs vs. overhead?

RRFF is in its founding season, so formal annual reporting will follow after the first completed reporting year. Current giving is processed through the Central Texas Community Foundation in partnership with Network for Good.

Can I direct my gift to a specific program?

Yes. The current priority is launching The 1884 Fund. You may also give toward wellness, community events, or where most needed once the giving flow is configured.

How do I cancel or change a monthly gift?

Email us at hello@roundrockfirefoundation.org with the email used to give. We process changes within two business days, no questions asked.

Does my employer match?

Many do. Add a matching-gift lookup widget or a list of common Round Rock employers that match. Replace this answer with your final flow.

How can I give in honor or memory of someone?

Add a note in the giving flow with the honoree’s name. We’ll send an acknowledgment card to the family at the address you provide.
